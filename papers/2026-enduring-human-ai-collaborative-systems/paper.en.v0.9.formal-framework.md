# Beyond Models: Toward Enduring Human–AI Collaborative Systems

## v0.9 — Formal Framework

## 1. Purpose

This version introduces a more formal representation of the Human–AI Collaborative System (HACS) framework. The objective is not to reduce collaboration to a mathematical formula, but to provide a structured model for research, evaluation, and future implementation.

## 2. Human–AI Collaborative System Definition

A Human–AI Collaborative System can be represented as an evolving system composed of multiple interacting dimensions:

HACS = f(H, A, C, G, R)

Where:

- H = Human expertise, judgment, values, and domain knowledge
- A = AI capability, reasoning, and computational capacity
- C = Context continuity accumulated through interaction
- G = Governance mechanisms and shared principles
- R = Relationship dynamics and collaboration quality

The system capability is not assumed to be equal to the sum of its components. Instead, emergent properties may arise from their interaction.

## 3. From Components to Emergence

Traditional AI evaluation focuses primarily on model capability:

Model Capability → Task Performance

This framework proposes an additional research direction:

Human Capability + AI Capability + Sustained Collaboration → Emergent Collaborative Intelligence

## 4. Core Dimensions

### Capability Layer

Measures what human and AI participants can contribute independently.

### Context Layer

Represents accumulated understanding, history, goals, constraints, and shared knowledge.

### Character Layer

Represents stable interaction patterns, professional alignment, and consistent collaborative behavior.

### Governance Layer

Defines boundaries, responsibility, transparency, and decision principles.

### Collaboration Layer

Measures the quality and sustainability of the partnership itself.

## 5. Research Proposition

The central proposition of this framework is:

Future AI progress should not be measured only by increasingly capable models, but also by increasingly effective collaborative systems.

## 6. Scientific Boundary

This framework does not claim that AI possesses human consciousness, intention, or emotion. Concepts such as character and commitment are used as architectural and behavioral constructs for designing reliable collaboration.

## 7. Future Work

Future research should investigate:

- measurable indicators of collaborative intelligence
- methods for evaluating long-term Human–AI partnerships
- governance mechanisms for critical domains
- failure recovery in collaborative systems
- applications in healthcare, research, education, and creative industries
