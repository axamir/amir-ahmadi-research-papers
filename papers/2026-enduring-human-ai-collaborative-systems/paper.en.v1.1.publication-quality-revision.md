# Beyond Models: Toward Enduring Human–AI Collaborative Systems

## Publication Quality Revision v1.1

### Scientific Positioning

This revision positions Human–AI Collaborative Systems within the broader landscape of Artificial Intelligence research.

The proposed framework does not replace existing approaches such as Human-Centered AI, AI Alignment, Responsible AI, or Autonomous Agents. Instead, it introduces an additional unit of analysis: the long-term collaborative system that emerges between humans and AI.

## Research Contribution

Current AI research often evaluates intelligence through model capability, task completion, and benchmark performance. This framework proposes that future systems should also be evaluated through the quality and stability of collaboration over time.

The central research question is:

> How can we design AI systems that do not only perform tasks, but participate in reliable, governed, and continuously improving collaboration with humans?

## Comparative Positioning

| Approach | Primary Focus |
| --- | --- |
| AI Agent | Autonomous task execution |
| AI Assistant | Human support and interaction |
| Human-Centered AI | Designing AI around human needs |
| AI Alignment | Ensuring intended behavior |
| Human–AI Collaborative System | Long-term mutual capability development |

## Core Hypothesis

The capability of a future AI system may depend not only on the intelligence of its underlying model, but also on the architecture of the collaboration surrounding that model.

## Scientific Boundary

This framework does not claim that AI possesses human consciousness, emotions, or intrinsic motivation. Terms such as character and commitment refer to designed behavioral and governance structures that support reliable collaboration.

## Future Work

Future research should investigate measurable properties including:

- Context Continuity
- Trust Calibration
- Collaborative Stability
- Decision Support Quality
- Human Agency Preservation

This document remains an open research proposal intended for experimentation, criticism, and collaborative improvement.
