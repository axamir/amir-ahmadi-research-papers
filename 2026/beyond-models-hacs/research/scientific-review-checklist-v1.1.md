# Scientific Review Checklist — v1.1.0 Preparation

## Purpose

This document defines the final scientific review process before preparing the v1.1.0 research release.

The goal is not to increase length, but to improve clarity, precision, attribution, and research credibility.

---

## 1. Claim Review

### Verify that:

- Research hypotheses are clearly separated from established findings.
- Conceptual proposals are not presented as proven theories.
- No claim of artificial consciousness or machine personhood is made.
- HACS is positioned as a proposed framework.

---

## 2. Terminology Review

Review consistency of key terms:

- Human–AI Collaborative System (HACS)
- Context Continuity
- Collaborative Intelligence
- Human Agency Preservation
- Trust Calibration
- Governance
- Provenance
- Recovery Capability

Each term should have:

- Definition
- Scope
- Limitations
- Relationship to existing research

---

## 3. Evidence and Attribution Review

Separate:

- Research evidence
- Design input
- Practitioner perspective
- Personal observation
- Future hypothesis

Human contributions must preserve fair attribution and avoid overstating roles.

---

## 4. Literature Alignment Review

Confirm positioning against:

- Human-Centered AI
- Human-AI Teaming
- Agentic AI
- AI Alignment
- Cognitive Systems
- Responsible AI Governance

The contribution should be described as integration and extension, not replacement of existing fields.

---

## 5. Framework Review

Check that HACS constructs have:

- Clear definitions
- Proposed relationships
- Potential evaluation methods
- Possible failure conditions

---

## 6. Language Review

Improve:

- Scientific tone
- Avoidance of exaggerated language
- Clear distinction between narrative origin and research validation
- Accessibility for interdisciplinary readers

---

## 7. Publication Readiness Criteria

Before v1.1.0 release:

- Paper updated
- README updated
- Changelog updated
- Citation metadata updated
- Release notes prepared
- DOI preparation considered

---

## Review Principle

The objective is not to prove HACS before evidence exists.

The objective is to present a clear, testable, and responsible research framework that invites critique, collaboration, and future validation.
