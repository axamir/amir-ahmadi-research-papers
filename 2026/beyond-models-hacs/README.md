## A Research Framework for the Future of Human–AI Collaboration

**Author:** Amir Ahmadi  
**Version:** v1.0.0 — Initial Research Release  
**Year:** 2026

---

## Overview

Artificial intelligence has traditionally been evaluated through model capability: accuracy, scale, reasoning, and automation.

This research explores a broader question:

> How can humans and AI systems build reliable, responsible, and meaningful collaboration over time?

**Human–AI Collaborative Systems (HACS)** is a proposed framework for studying long-term collaboration between human expertise and artificial intelligence capability.

---

## Core Idea

HACS views future AI not only as a collection of powerful models, but as part of a socio-technical system built around:

- Human expertise
- AI capability
- Context continuity
- Governance mechanisms
- Feedback and adaptation

---

## Position

This work does not claim that current AI systems possess consciousness, emotions, or human intentions.

It proposes a research direction for designing systems where AI can support human capability while preserving human responsibility, creativity, and judgment.
